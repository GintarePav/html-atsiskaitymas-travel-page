My Trips

Visiting New York in Winter

New York City transforms into a winter wonderland during the colder months, offering visitors a magical experience filled with festive cheer and iconic attractions blanketed in snow. You can see Gapstow Bridge in Central Park in the photo above.

January, 2019

As the city lights twinkle against the backdrop of falling snowflakes, exploring the bustling streets becomes an adventure in itself. From ice skating at Rockefeller Center to marveling at the dazzling displays along Fifth Avenue, there's no shortage of enchanting activities to partake in.

One can't miss the opportunity to witness the breathtaking views of Manhattan from atop the Empire State Building, where the cityscape takes on a serene charm amidst the winter chill. Afterward, warm up with a cup of hot chocolate from a cozy café in Greenwich Village or indulge in a hearty bowl of ramen in Chinatown. For those seeking cultural experiences, Broadway theaters come alive with captivating performances, while world-class museums like the Metropolitan Museum of Art offer refuge from the cold with their captivating exhibits.

As the day winds down, Central Park emerges as a picturesque winter oasis, inviting visitors to stroll along snow-covered paths and admire the tranquil beauty of frozen lakes and snow-dusted trees. Whether it's taking in the panoramic views from the Bow Bridge or enjoying a horse-drawn carriage ride through the park, New York City in the winter offers a magical escape that captivates the senses and creates lasting memories for all who embrace its snowy charm.

Visiting Tokyo in Spring

Embarking on a journey to Tokyo in the spring promises a captivating experience as the city bursts into a kaleidoscope of colors with cherry blossoms in full bloom. In the photo above you can see the part of the city near Meguro Station where you can best enjoy cherry blossoms.

May, 2019

From late March to early April, sakura season blankets Tokyo's parks, streets, and riverbanks in delicate pink petals, creating a breathtaking backdrop for exploration. Strolling through iconic spots like Shinjuku Gyoen, Ueno Park, and the Meguro River, visitors are enveloped in a serene ambiance as they witness the fleeting beauty of cherry blossoms, known as hanami, celebrated through picnics and festivities.

Beyond the enchanting spectacle of cherry blossoms, Tokyo in the spring offers a wealth of cultural and culinary delights to discover. Immerse yourself in the bustling energy of neighborhoods like Shibuya and Harajuku, where trendy boutiques, quirky cafes, and vibrant street fashion converge. Indulge your taste buds with seasonal delicacies, from fresh sushi and sashimi to traditional wagashi sweets adorned with edible cherry blossoms. For a dose of traditional culture, explore historic landmarks such as Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa or the tranquil gardens of the Imperial Palace.

As the days grow longer and the temperatures rise, Tokyo's outdoor spaces come alive with a myriad of activities and events. Join locals and visitors alike in Hanami festivals, where lively music, food stalls, and illuminated night views enhance the cherry blossom experience. Take in panoramic views of the city from observation decks like Tokyo Skytree or the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building, offering unparalleled vistas of Tokyo's skyline adorned with blooming cherry blossoms. With its blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and modern allure, Tokyo in the spring captivates the senses and promises an unforgettable journey filled with moments of wonder and discovery.

Visiting London in Summer

Visiting London in the summer unveils a vibrant tapestry of cultural richness and outdoor festivities against the backdrop of sunny skies and balmy temperatures. In the photo above you can see Camden's famous market, which hosts evening street food markets every Thursday.

August, 2019

From June to August, the city bursts with life as parks, gardens, and historic landmarks become the stage for a plethora of events and activities. Hyde Park transforms into a hub of entertainment with outdoor concerts and theater performances, while the South Bank teems with bustling markets, riverside cafes, and open-air cinemas, offering a perfect blend of leisure and excitement.

Exploring London's iconic attractions takes on a new charm in the summer months, with longer daylight hours and warmer weather beckoning travelers to discover the city's treasures. From the majestic Tower of London and Buckingham Palace to the eclectic Camden Market and the renowned British Museum, each corner of the city offers a glimpse into its rich history, diverse culture, and vibrant creativity. Embark on a leisurely stroll along the River Thames or hop on a boat cruise to admire the city's landmarks from a different perspective, soaking in the beauty of London's skyline illuminated by the golden glow of the setting sun.

Summer in London is also synonymous with a culinary adventure, as the city's eclectic dining scene comes alive with seasonal flavors and al fresco dining options. From traditional pubs serving refreshing pints of locally brewed beer to trendy rooftop bars offering panoramic views of the city, there's no shortage of gastronomic delights to savor. Sample global cuisines at food festivals like Taste of London or feast on classic British dishes at outdoor markets, where stalls brim with artisanal treats and gourmet delights. With its blend of cultural allure, outdoor splendor, and culinary delights, London in the summer invites visitors to immerse themselves in a world of endless possibilities and unforgettable experiences.

Visiting Barcelona in Autumn

Visiting Barcelona in autumn unveils a captivating blend of cultural richness, culinary delights, and mild Mediterranean weather. In the photo above you can see the Montserrat monastery located in the mountains.

November, 2019

As the summer crowds disperse, the city takes on a more relaxed pace, making it an ideal time to explore its charming streets, vibrant neighborhoods, and iconic landmarks. From the architectural wonders of Antoni Gaudí, such as the Sagrada Familia and Park Güell, to the historic Gothic Quarter with its narrow alleys and medieval charm, Barcelona offers a treasure trove of cultural experiences waiting to be discovered.

Autumn in Barcelona also brings a culinary feast for the senses, with local markets brimming with seasonal produce and traditional Catalan dishes. From hearty stews and seafood paellas to savory tapas and sweet churros dipped in rich chocolate, the city's gastronomic scene showcases the best of Mediterranean cuisine. Explore the bustling food markets of La Boqueria and Santa Caterina, where vendors display an array of fresh fruits, cheeses, olives, and cured meats, providing a true taste of Catalan flavors.

As the temperatures begin to cool, Barcelona's natural beauty comes alive with the changing colors of autumn foliage. Take a leisurely stroll along the city's scenic waterfront promenade or escape to the tranquil oasis of Montjuïc Hill, where lush gardens and panoramic views of the city await. For outdoor enthusiasts, the nearby Montserrat mountain range offers hiking trails surrounded by breathtaking landscapes of rugged peaks and golden vineyards, providing a perfect retreat into nature just a short distance from the bustling city center. Whether indulging in cultural experiences, culinary adventures, or outdoor escapades, Barcelona in autumn captivates the heart and soul with its unique blend of beauty, flavor, and charm.
